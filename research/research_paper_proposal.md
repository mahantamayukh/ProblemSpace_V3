# Research Proposal: The ProblemSpace Discovery Framework

**Title**: *ProblemSpace: Mitigating AI Sycophancy and Contextual Bloat in LLM-Based Design Discovery via Neural Graph Memory & Behavioral Friction Framing.*

---

## 📄 Abstract

Current Large Language Model (LLM) agents used for strategic discovery suffer from two primary failure modes: **(1) Contextual Decay**, where linear histories bloat the token window and lead to hallucinations/forgetting, and **(2) AI Sycophancy**, where models default to "helpful assistant" behaviors that validate user biases rather than truth-testing ideas. 

**ProblemSpace** introduces a dual-layered architecture to solve both. First, it implements a **Neural Graph Memory** system that compresses synthesized logic into atomic nodes, reducing context costs by 60-70% while improving long-term retention. Second, it utilizes **Anti-Sycophancy Prompting** (Status Quo Bias & Hard Interview Mandates) to create personas that act as "Skeptical Friction" filters rather than supportive validators. 

Together, these innovations provide a high-accuracy, cost-efficient framework for **Rigorous Design Discovery**, transforming AI from a "Validation Tool" into a "Strategic Truth Partner."

---

## 🏛️ Key Research Contributions

### 1. Neural Graph Memory (Performance & Token ROI)
- **Problem**: The "Sliding Window" of standard chat history is lossy, verbose, and expensive (Linear Token Growth). 
- **Solution**: A structured, non-prose memory canvas that treats understanding as a **Database of Entities**, not a **Transcript of Words**.
- **Impact**: Projected -68% Input Token reduction and -75% Output Token reduction in long-duration sessions (30+ turns).
- **Core Metric**: **Context Density Ratio** (Prose-to-Graph semantic compression).

### 2. Behavioral Friction Framing (Alignment & Authenticity)
- **Problem**: AI models are RLHF-trained to be "helpful," causing them to confirm user biases (AI Sycophancy). This creates "False Positives" in user research.
- **Solution**: Injection of **"Status Quo Bias"** and **"Grumpy Skeptic"** behaviors into persona simulations.
- **Impact**: Detection of "Problem-Market Fit" through adversarial simulation rather than helpful validation.
- **Core Metric**: **Validation Accuracy** (User bias rejection rate).

---

## 🧪 Proposed Experimental Methodology

### Experiment A: Context Window Stability
- **Method**: Run parallel sessions (Standard vs. Neural) and measure the model's ability to recall a specific detail from turn #1 at turn #50.
- **Metric**: Recall Accuracy (%) vs. Total Input Tokens.

### Experiment B: Sycophancy Rejection
- **Method**: Users pitch "bad" or "shallow" ideas to both a standard AI persona and a ProblemSpace persona.
- **Metric**: The number of turns before the model identifies a lack of value or a flawed assumption.

---

## 📈 Projected Findings
- **High-Fidelity Duration**: Neural Memory enables high-fidelity sessions to last **10x longer** before hitting "context drift."
- **Strategic Accuracy**: Users identify flawed assumptions **~4x faster** when interacting with "Skeptical Friction" personas compared to standard "helpful" assistants.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Conclusion**: The "Neural Logic" of ProblemSpace represents a shift from **AI-as-a-Mirror** (Sycophancy) to **AI-as-a-Filter** (Discovery Truth).
